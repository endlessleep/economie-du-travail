11. Active Labour Market Policies (ALMP)

🧠 I. The Structural Trap: Long-Term Unemployment (LTU)

Goal of ALMPs: Increase employment opportunities, return job seekers to work faster, and improve matching. Targeted groups include the long-term unemployed (>12 months), youth, elderly, low-skilled, and disabled.

Economic Shock ➔ Job Loss ➔ Time Passes ➔ Skills Deteriorate & Motivation Drops ➔ LTU Trap

🚨 Why Raw Unemployment Rates are Misleading

Not all unemployment is equal. A country can have a low unemployment rate but a high share of LTU. This means that while few people are out of work, those who are remain trapped for extended periods.

  • Short-term unemployment = Frictional, normal job searching.
  • Long-term unemployment (LTU) = Structural breakdown. The matching process between workers and jobs is broken.

Cumulative Disadvantage

The longer you are unemployed, the harder it is to find a job because:

  • Skills deteriorate.
  • Motivation declines (Discouraged worker effect).
  • Stigma: Employers view LTU as a negative signal.

The Antidote: ALMP

Economic growth alone cannot fix LTU. We need Active Labour Market Policies (ALMPs) to intervene directly by:

  • Re-skilling workers.
  • Subsidizing hiring to offset employer stigma.
  • Improving search efficiency.

🛠️ II. ALMP Tools & The Budget Dilemma

ALMPs encompass all institutional interventions aimed at enhancing employment and labour market participation.

The 5 Core Tools of ALMP

  • 1. Public Employment Services (PES): (Úřad práce) Reduces search frictions by directly matching vacancies with suitable workers.
  • 2. Training: Upgrades human capital to fix skill mismatches and improve employability.
  • 3. Subsidized Employment (SUPM): Provides financial incentives to firms hiring disadvantaged workers, reducing perceived employer risks.
  • 4. Public Works (VPP): Direct job creation by the government to maintain a worker's attachment to the labour market.
  • 5. Start-up Incentives: Support for entrepreneurship (credit, tax discounts, legal help).
💰 The Expenditure Trade-off: Passive vs. Active

Labour market budgets are split into two categories. Governments face a persistent tension between short-term welfare protection and long-term efficiency:

  • Passive Policies (PLMP): Unemployment benefits. They stabilize income and prevent poverty, but do not solve structural unemployment.
  • Active Policies (ALMP): The 5 tools above. They modify the structure of the labour market to reintegrate workers.
The Benefit Design Mechanism:

High Initial Replacement Rate (Income Security)
➔
Declining Benefits Over Time (Forces Job Search Incentive)

📐 III. Theory: Equilibrium Unemployment (Layard-Nickell)

Why does unemployment exist even when the economy is stable? The Layard-Nickell (1986) model shows that unemployment is not just a lack of demand, but is built into the structure of how wages are negotiated.

1. The Two Forces

Labour Demand (Downward): Higher wages mean higher costs, so firms hire less.

Wage-Setting (Upward): When employment is high, labour is scarce. Workers have bargaining power and demand higher wages.

2. Equilibrium $A$ & Involuntary $u_0$

Point A: The market clears. Firms are happy to hire, workers are happy with the wage.

BUT... Point A is to the left of the "Full Employment" line! The distance is involuntary unemployment ($u_0$). Workers want to work at that wage, but firms won't hire them.

💡 Key Takeaway

Unemployment is a structural equilibrium. Wages don't drop enough to clear the market due to bargaining and institutions. Economic growth isn't enough; we need ALMPs to shift these curves!

🔄 IV. The Modified Model: Gross vs. Net Effects

To evaluate ALMP, we must split the labour force into three groups:

$e$ = Regular Employment $u$ = Open Unemployment $r$ = Programme Participation

⚠️ The Ultimate Evaluation Trap

When someone joins an ALMP program, they move from $u$ to $r$. The official unemployment rate drops. This is the Gross Effect.

BUT BE CAREFUL! Just because unemployment dropped doesn't mean employment ($e$) increased! They just changed statistical categories. ALMPs are only successful if the "Net Effect" is positive: participants must eventually transition into stable, regular employment ($e$).

🧩 V. The 5 Indirect Effects of ALMP

ALMPs shift the structural curves of the labour market. The total Net Effect depends on these 5 indirect mechanisms.

1. Matching Efficiency (Positive Effect)

Training and PES help workers find the right jobs faster.

Matching ↑ ➔ Firms hire faster & easier ➔ Wage pressure ↓ AND Labour demand ↑ ➔ Regular Employment ↑

2. Competition Effects (Positive Effect)

ALMPs keep people actively searching, increasing the number of active job seekers.

More active seekers ➔ Competition for jobs ↑ ➔ Workers accept lower wages ➔ Wage-setting ↓ ➔ Regular Employment ↑

3. Productivity Effects (Ambiguous Effect)

ALMPs improve skills, but this has a contradictory effect.

  • Scale Effect: Higher productivity = cheaper unit costs = expand production = Employment ↑.
  • Substitution Effect: More efficient workers = fewer workers needed = Employment ↓.
Productivity ↑ ➔ Scale vs. Substitution Effect + Wage pressure ↑ ➔ Employment Ambiguous

4. Reduced Welfare Loss (Negative Effect)

If ALMP programs are "too comfortable" (good pay, low risk), unemployment becomes less painful.

Welfare loss ↓ ➔ Reservation wage ↑ ➔ Wage-setting shifts UP ➔ Regular Employment ↓

5. Deadweight & Substitution (Negative Effect)

The biggest problem in practice (70-90% of ALMP "jobs"):

  • Deadweight: The firm gets a subsidy to hire someone they were going to hire anyway.
  • Substitution: The firm fires an unsubsidized worker to hire the subsidized one.
Zero "Additionality" ➔ Labour demand for non-participants ↓ ➔ Net Employment Effect Tiny/Negative

🔬 VI. Empirical Evaluation: Does it Work in Practice?

We evaluate ALMPs via Macroeconomic studies (aggregate rates) and Experimental studies (Comparing treated participants vs. a control group). Let's look at the Czech Republic project "Nové pracovní příležitosti" (SUPM vs. VPP).

📉 The Decaying Effect

Right after leaving a program, employment spikes! But after 12-18 months, the effect decays. Many participants return to unemployment once the subsidized contract ends. ALMPs trigger short-term transitions, but struggle to guarantee durable integration.

🧩 Target Group Heterogeneity

ALMPs are NOT one-size-fits-all:

  • Youth: Highly effective (they just lack experience).
  • Parents: Need childcare, not just training.
  • Disabled/Older: Face deep structural/discriminatory barriers. ALMP alone is insufficient.

💡 EXAM-READY CONCLUSION

ALMPs are useful activation tools, but they are not a universal panacea.

  1. They work best for individuals close to the labour market (e.g., youth).
  2. They struggle with groups facing cumulative disadvantages (disabled, socially excluded).
  3. The ultimate test is "Net Effect": ALMPs must create additionality and forge stable transitions into regular employment ($e$), not just temporarily hide people in statistical programs ($r$).
