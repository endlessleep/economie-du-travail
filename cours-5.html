Labour Supply: The Basic Neoclassical Model

⏳ I. Basic Neoclassical Model of Labour Supply

The neoclassical model of labor supply analyzes how individuals decide to allocate their fixed amount of time between work (labor) and non-work (leisure).

1. Fundamental Assumptions

  • The Labour-Leisure Trade-off: There are only two possible uses of time: labour and leisure.
  • Utility Maximization: Each individual selects the combination of hours of work and leisure that maximizes his or her level of satisfaction (utility).

2. Demand for Leisure

Leisure is treated as a normal good. Its demand depends on:

  • Preferences: Personal tastes and subjective value of time.
  • Wealth/Income: Higher income generally leads to more hours of leisure ($\uparrow Y \implies \uparrow L$).
  • Opportunity Costs: The cost of leisure is the wage rate ($W$) given up.

Initial Labour-Leisure Choice

The starting point of the individual's time allocation decision.

🧠 II. Preferences and Utility

1. Indifference Curves ($U = U(Y, L)$)

An indifference curve represents all combinations of Real Income ($Y$) and Leisure ($L$) that give the same level of satisfaction.

  • Downward Sloping: Reflects the trade-off between income and leisure.
  • Convex to the origin: As leisure increases, the individual is willing to give up less income for an additional hour of leisure (Diminishing Marginal Rate of Substitution).
  • Higher curves = Higher utility.

Comparison of Leisure Preferences

Steep curves indicate a high value on leisure (requires more income to give it up), while flatter curves indicate a lower relative value.

2. The Constraints

Time Constraint

Total time available ($T$) is split between Hours of work ($H$) and Leisure ($L$).

\[ H + L = T \]

Goods/Budget Constraint

Consumption ($pY$) equals labour income ($wH$) plus any non-labour income ($V$).

\[ pY = wH + V \]

Time Allocation Graph

Budget Constraint Slope

The slope of the budget line is $-w/p$ (the real wage).

3. Optimization: The Hours of Work Decision

The individual reaches the optimal choice where the indifference curve is tangent to the budget line ($\text{MRS}_{L,Y} = w$).

⚖️ III. Substitution and Income Effects

Observed Change in Hours

The total change in hours worked when the wage increases is the sum of two effects.

1. Substitution Effect (SE)

$\uparrow W \implies$ Leisure is more expensive $\implies \downarrow L$ and $\uparrow H$.

2. Income Effect (IE)

$\uparrow W \implies$ Higher real income $\implies \uparrow L$ and $\downarrow H$ (because leisure is normal).

Net Effect: More Work

Strong SE ($SE > IE$).

Net Effect: More Leisure

Strong IE ($IE > SE$).

🏃 V. Participation Decision & Reservation Wage

Corner Solutions

Occurs when the individual reaches maximum utility by not working at all ($H=0$).

Reservation Wage ($W_R$)

The minimum wage needed to tempt someone to work. If $W_{market} < W_R$, they won't participate.

Market Entry decision

Wages below Reservation

💰 VI. Impact of Non-Labour Income

Non-labour income ($V$) shifts the budget line vertically without changing its slope.

Theoretical Result: Since leisure is a normal good, an increase in $V$ leads to a pure Income Effect $\implies \uparrow L$ and $\downarrow H$.

⚖️ VII. Income Replacement & Welfare Programs

Public policies significantly alter the shape of the budget constraint and work incentives.

1. Basic Welfare Program ($Y_t$)

Guarantees a minimum income but reduces benefits \$1 for \$1 for every dollar earned. This creates a Marginal Wage of zero.

Impact on Supply

Reduces work hours for most recipients and can lead to labour market exit.

Exit Decision

At current wages, the utility from the welfare benefit is higher than from working.

Summary: General Welfare Effect

2. Policy Variants: Workfare & UBI

💡 Exam Strategy Summary

  • Substitution Effect: Always moves hours in the same direction as the wage.
  • Income Effect: Moves hours in the opposite direction.
  • Reservation Wage: If the market wage is below it, optimal work $H=0$.
  • Welfare Trapezoids: Pay close attention to the kinks in the budget line caused by benefit phase-outs!
